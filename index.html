THE Word "I"

Religion is a philosophy to live your life by.

Everything is a constant change. You have no intrinsic identity that is seperate from the things going on around you. The moment defines your identity. The main foundation of suffering is you believe that you have a single, consistent unchanging core 'self' that uniquely defines you. Describe yourself? Self depends on context. It's normal to be friendly in one situation, shy in another and rude in third. When you cling to the word I, I am this and I am that, that is when you set up miserable life. That's when you crave wealth and power. With the word 'I' comes desire and selfishness.
Everything in this world is held together by relationship. Relationship with your family, friends, boyfriend, girlfriend. With the relationship comes the most intriguing word love. Love that drives the world. There is two kind of love in this world conditional love and unconditional love. Conditional love says, I love you so I want you. Unconditional love says, I love you so I want you to be happy. The key to happiness is letting go of I and embracing the unconditional love.
This all sounds ridiculous right. How can one let go 'I' and love unconditionally? You can, for example if you encounter an invasive tulip in your vegetable garden, you might be tempted to rip it out of the ground, or pick it to give to your lover. You’re seeing the tulip through your own needs and desires, which are bound up with your illusory idea of self. To see the tulip as it truly is, you must let go of self-focused stories about the tulip – how it doesn’t belong there, or how much your lover would like it – and experience the tulip in a way that’s unrelated to your own needs. Notice its beautiful colour. Be awed by the power of nature. Experience the irony of a flower thriving among the legumes. Appreciate the tulip because it’s there, not because you’re there. The central idea of the freedom and happiness is to let go.

Let go of I and embrace the change, this is a key facet of wisdom.
